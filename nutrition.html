Nutrition

Diet

  • Maintaining good health and feeling your best can both be facilitated by eating a healthy, balanced diet.According to the NHS in England, there are a few steps that can be followed to achieve a healthier life.
  • Firstly Introduce foods that are starchy carbohydrates, such as potatos, wholewheat pasta, which contains fiber.
  • Secondly, eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, that offer vitamins and minerals as well as fibre. Eat oily fish, to ensure you benefit from Omega 3.
  • Reduce your intake of saturated fats and sugar and salt.

Hydration

  • The recommended intake of fluids is the consumption of 6-8 mugs of liquids, such as water, juice or diluted squash, the main message is to drink plenty throughout the day.
  • Other factors influence how much liquid you should consume, for instance, the weather or if you are being active, therefore increase your fluids.
  • Being sick, vomiting or suffering from diarrhorea also influences your chances of becoming dehydrate, so its highly importante to drink more.

Suplements

  • Supplementation is not a substitute for a healthy diet, however, there are instances where adding vitamins and supplements might be necessary.
  • Certain groups of people may need to supplement their diets, pregnant women, older adults, and people following stricter diets may benefit from supplements.
  • Discuss with your doctor before taking supplements, follow the instructions on the label and read all the ingredients the supplements contain.
